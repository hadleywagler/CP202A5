About Us


We are upper year students who attend Wilfrid Laurier University. Combined we have a strong variety of University experience and knowledge. We created this website with the goal to inform fellow Laurier students about EVERYTHING this school has to offer, both academically and socially. Transitioning to a large institution like Laurier usually overwhelms first years. We want to bring expert knowledge regarding day-to-day life at Laurier and the Waterloo community. We hope by doing this that people will maximize their time and not miss anything.

Everyone has a place at Laurier, and this is why we intend to target any student from 1st to 5th year. This website will inform every viewer on something new or at least promote specific things. Our content is mainly constructed from upper year students, and most of the advice is aimed at helping 1st and 2nd year students. It will not be specific to a certain program or age, and anyone attending Laurier or even in the Waterloo community will have access to this website and our resources will teach you more about life at WLU. Overall we want to be a popular online page were students can go if they have any problems or concerns regarding anything at Laurier. We will also offer a contact us page where students can provide their own advice as well as express misunderstandings with University life.

How to Study at Laurier

What type of learner are you?

One of the first and most important tips for success in university is to understand the way in which you learn best. There are three different types of learners: visual, auditory and kinesthetic. Once you have determined which type of learner you are, taking action in how you study and using these methods are crucial.

Textbooks

Laurier students should be aware that there are other ways to access textbooks rather than paying full price at the bookstore. One way to do this is to buy used from the Laurier facebook pages available for students to join. One tip though is to look at the price in the bookstore and if there is an access code that you have to purchase for the course. Best Study Locations

According to a recent survey conducted, Laurier students find the best study locations on campus are the library, the science atrium and in Lazaridis, the new business building.

Best Study Times

When it comes to finding the right time to claim your spot in one of these study areas, the earlier in the day you can make it the better. Especially during exams, if you want space on campus to study, going to sleep earlier so you can get up early is the best way to approach finding a seat.

How to stay Healthy at Laurier

Healthy Eating

Healthy eating is another vital aspect of your physical health. This is one of those things that can be hard to accomplish because it can take up quite a bit of time and energy to do effectively. A great way to have a handle on this would be to meal prep. This creates a way for you to have healthy meals prepped in advance and ready to eat when you are hungry.

Mental Health

The next huge part of staying healthy while studying is being aware of your mental health. This is something that people tend to overlook and push through while in school because they think that grades are more important. This is NOT true. There are many different resources available for Laurier students, such as the health and wellness centre, that can help in times of distress.

Physical Health

Laurier is one of those schools that is well known for making physical activity a priority. There are so many different ways to make this happen; working out at the gym, participating in intramural sports, taking group exercise classes and so many more.

How to be Social at Laurier

Having a structured academic life as well as a social life is extremely important while living at Laurier. This is the first time most students are living on their own, and it is essential to surround yourself with people that will make you happy and help you to achieve your goals. Doing well in school is obviously the main priority but making lasting connections at university is a once in a lifetime opportunity.

Key Social Events at Laurier

O-Week is a must attend event as a first year! There are also many activities that ae hosted by the school as well as outside promoters to engage upper year student's in the O-Week tradition. O-Week is held during the first week of school every fall semester.

Intramural sports, as mentioned before are a great way to meet people, these are put on by Laurier Athletics.

Clubs are an easy way to meet people and inform you on topics regarding real world concerns. There is also opportunity at Laurier to start your own club.

For upper year students, there are prized night clubs as well as bars that most students recommend!

Laurier campus is your new home, and we want you to feel safe, secure and especially want you to have fun. We hope that these ideas get you started with your social journey here at Laurier!!!
